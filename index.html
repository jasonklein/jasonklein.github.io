trellisd

Providing a solution to the question, “Does anyone know anyone who…?”, trellisd is a Rails app that matches posts from users with complementary posts (e.g., posts seeking work find posts offering work) from others in users’ extended networks, based on category, directionality, and keywords. trellisd is responsive, written primarily in HAML and SCSS on its front-end, incorporates jQuery UI and the Facebook Graph API, and uses, among other gems, High Voltage, Kaminari, Bourbon, Neat, and Bitters.

trellisd matches posts from users with complementary posts from others in users’ extended networks, based on category, directionality, and keywords.

  • Heroku

Stacksy

Stacksy is a dating platform for tech workers to "pour a little syntactic sugar" on their love lives. Users have profiles, interact by sending messages and “pings”, and can pay for additional features. Stacksy is a Rails app, is written primarily in HAML on its front-end, and incorporates JavaScript, AJAX, Omniauth for Facebook, and custom-built private messaging functionality. Geocoder, for location-based searching, and Devise, among other gems, are used in Stacksy. Co-built with another General Assembly student.

Stacksy is a dating platform for tech workers to "pour a little syntactic sugar" on their love lives

Gai.ly

A course management app, Gai.ly, built with Rails, enables General Assembly to add courses to its catalogue, book those courses into classrooms, and schedule instructors and students in those courses. Gai.ly has custom user authentication and incorporates CanCan and Ransack, among other gems.

Gai.ly is a course booking, enrollment, and management app.
