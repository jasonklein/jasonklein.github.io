About

I am a web developer and a recent graduate of General Assembly's Web Development Immersive course in London. I am seeking a position in an organization where I can apply my web development, client-management, problem-solving and other skills to meaningfully advance such organization’s goals.

I am a fan of city cycling, podcasts (in particular, The Web Ahead, Throwing Shade, and Pop Culture Happy Hour), Monocle, films that show teamwork, Manhattan's that are on the sweeter side (which appreciation might also be part of why I enjoy using the Bourbon, Neat, and Bitters CSS framework so much), and breakfast.

My background is in law, having practiced as a finance and securities attorney for a multinational white-shoe law firm in its offices in New York, Paris, and London.

One day (truly, on one day), I realized that I wanted to spend my time solving more creative problems more creatively and, after careful consideration, decided to shift into web development.

Projects

The below are my most significant projects to date.

trellisd

Providing a solution to the question, “Does anyone know anyone who…?”, trellisd matches posts from users with complementary posts (e.g., posts seeking work find posts offering work) from others in users’ extended networks, based on category, directionality, and keywords. trellisd is responsive, written primarily in HAML and SCSS, incorporates jQuery UI and the Facebook Graph API, and uses, among other gems, High Voltage, Kaminari, Bourbon, Neat, and Bitters.

trellisd matches posts from users with complementary posts from others in users’ extended networks, based on category, directionality, and keywords.

Stacksy

Stacksy is a dating platform for tech workers to "pour a little syntactic sugar" on their love lives. Users have profiles, interact by sending messages and “pings”, and can pay for additional features. Stacksy is written primarily in HAML and incorporates JavaScript, AJAX, the Facebook Graph API, and custom-built private messaging functionality. Geocoder, for location-based searching, and Devise, among other gems, are used in Stacksy. Co-built with another General Assembly student.

Stacksy is a dating platform for tech workers to "pour a little syntactic sugar" on their love lives

Gai.ly

A course management app, Gai.ly enables General Assembly to add courses to its catalogue, book those courses into classrooms, and schedule instructors and students in those courses. Gai.ly has custom user authentication and incorporates CanCan and Ransack, among other gems.

Gai.ly is a course booking, enrollment, and management app.

Disastronomia

Disastronomia is a cookbook of epic proportions, where users can search and write recipes for serving disaster, apocalyptic ruin, and pandemonium. Disastronomia has custom user authentication, and incorporates CanCan and Ransack, among other gems.
